Bhagat Singh (Born: 28 September 1907[a] , Martyrdom: 23 March 1931) was a great freedom fighter and revolutionary of India. Along with Chandrashekhar Azad and other party members, he fought the powerful British government with unprecedented courage for India's independence. First the assassination of Bernie Sanders in Lahore and then the bomb blast in Delhi's Central Parliament (Central Assembly) raised open rebellion against the British Empire. He refused to run even after throwing a bomb in the assembly. As a result, the British government hanged him on March 23, 1931, along with his two other companions, Rajguru and Sukhdev.
